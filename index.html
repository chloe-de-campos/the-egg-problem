The Egg Problem

Here, have a dozen eggs.

The only catch? One of them has gone bad. Though the bad egg is visually identical to the other eggs, you know that its weight is marginally different than the weight of the other eggs. You have a balance scale which you may use 3 times to compare sets of eggs. Your challenge: find the bad egg and determine whether it is heavier or lighter than the other eggs.

I've labelled the eggs for you to tell them apart. Drag each egg to the boxes in the scale to compare their weights.The results of your previous measurements will appear on the left side. You have 3 uses of the scale to find the bad egg, before you'll have to confirm your guess.
